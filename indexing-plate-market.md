<p><p>Indexing plates are used in various industries for precise and efficient positioning of workpieces during machining operations. They can be categorized based on function, generality type, optical type, numerical control type, and structure. Function-based indexing plates serve specific purposes like dividing the workpiece into equal parts. Generality Type indexing plates are versatile and can be adjusted for different machining operations. Optical type indexing plates use optical sensors for high precision positioning. Numerical control type indexing plates are computer-controlled, ensuring accurate and automated operations. Structure-based indexing plates include four-axis turntables, enabling motion in four directions, and five-axis turntables, allowing movement in five directions. Other indexing plates offer unique features catering to specialized industries or machining needs.</p></p>
