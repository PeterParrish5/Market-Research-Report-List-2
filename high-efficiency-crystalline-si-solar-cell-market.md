<p><ul><li>BCSC (Buried Contact Solar Cell)</li><li>LFC (Laser Fired Contact)</li><li>HIT (Hetero-junction with Intrinsic Thin Layer)</li><li>Back Contact Solar Cell</li><li>Passivated Emitter Solar Cell</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>1. BCSC (Buried Contact Solar Cell): This type of high efficiency crystalline Si solar cell has the electrical contacts located beneath the surface, enhancing light absorption and reducing losses due to shading.</p><p>2. LFC (Laser Fired Contact): LFC solar cells use lasers to create contact points on the solar cell surface, improving current flow and reducing electrical resistance.</p><p>3. HIT (Hetero-junction with Intrinsic Thin Layer): HIT solar cells utilize a thin intrinsic layer in addition to a hetero-junction, increasing the efficiency of light absorption and reducing recombination losses.</p><p>4. Back Contact Solar Cell: In this type, the electrical contacts are located on the rear side of the solar cell, improving the overall efficiency by reducing shading and increasing the available active surface area.</p><p>5. Passivated Emitter Solar Cell: These solar cells have a passivating layer on the front side, reducing surface recombination and increasing the overall efficiency.</p><p>6. Others Market: This refers to other types of high efficiency crystalline Si solar cells that may feature unique design enhancements or combinations of different technologies, not specifically categorized under the aforementioned types.</p></p>
