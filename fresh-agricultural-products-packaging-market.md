<p><p>Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) involves altering the composition of gases within a package, typically by reducing oxygen levels and increasing levels of carbon dioxide or nitrogen, to extend the shelf life of fresh agricultural products. Vacuum Skin Packaging (VSP) utilizes a special film that is adhered to the product and sealed tightly to provide a protective barrier against external elements. Vacuum Thermoformed Packaging (VTP) involves using heat and vacuum to form custom trays or containers for products, offering a secure and hygienic packaging solution. Other market types may include innovative packaging solutions like active packaging or intelligent packaging.</p></p>
