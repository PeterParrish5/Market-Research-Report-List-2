<p><p>Modified starch is a widely used ingredient in the food market due to its ability to enhance texture, stability, and shelf life of various food products. There are several types of modified starches available in the market. Compound modified starch combines different forms of modification to achieve specific functionalities. Oxidized starch undergoes a chemical reaction to improve its thickening and gelling properties. Cationic starch is positively charged, making it effective for improving binding and moisture retention. Acetate starch is modified with acetic acid to enhance its heat resistance. Phosphate starch is treated with phosphoric acid, providing excellent freeze-thaw stability. Pregelatinized starch is cooked and dried, making it instantaneously soluble. Other modified starches offer various functionalities like improved mouthfeel, reduced fat absorption, and enhanced emulsion stability in food products.</p></p>
