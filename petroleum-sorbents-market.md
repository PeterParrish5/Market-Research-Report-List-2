<p><p>The petroleum sorbents market can be categorized into two types: inorganic sorbents and cellulose sorbents. Inorganic sorbents are made from amorphous or crystalline minerals, such as clay and silica gel, which have high absorption capacity for hydrocarbons. Cellulose sorbents, on the other hand, are derived from plant materials and have a natural affinity for oil and other petroleum products. Both types of sorbents are used to clean up and contain oil spills, leaks, and other accidents in order to prevent environmental contamination.</p></p>
