<p><p>Refrigeration fluids, also known as refrigerants, are substances used in refrigeration systems to transfer heat out of an enclosed space or object, thereby reducing its temperature. These fluids undergo a phase change from liquid to gas and back to liquid, absorbing heat during evaporation and releasing it during condensation. Common types of refrigeration fluids include chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFCs), hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), and natural refrigerants such as ammonia and carbon dioxide.</p><p>The refrigeration fluids market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years, mainly driven by the increasing demand for refrigeration and air conditioning systems across various industries. The rising disposable income and changing lifestyle patterns have led to a surge in the adoption of refrigeration equipment in residential and commercial sectors, further augmenting the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the stringent regulations and protocols implemented by governments and environmental organizations to curb greenhouse gas emissions and reduce ozone depletion have resulted in a shift towards eco-friendly refrigerants. This has fueled the demand for natural refrigerants such as ammonia and carbon dioxide, which are environmentally benign and have a lower global warming potential.</p><p>The market analysis reveals that the Asia-Pacific region dominates the global refrigeration fluids market, owing to the booming industrial and commercial sectors in countries like China and India.
