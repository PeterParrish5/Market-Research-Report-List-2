<p><p>The detector diode market can be divided into three types based on frequency range: high frequency, intermediate frequency, and low frequency. High-frequency detector diodes are designed for applications that involve signals with high frequencies, typically above 1 GHz. Intermediate-frequency detector diodes are suitable for moderate frequency ranges, usually between 30 MHz and 1 GHz. Low-frequency detector diodes are used for lower frequency applications, often below 30 MHz. These different types allow the detector diodes to be optimized for specific frequency bands and ensure better performance and accuracy in signal detection.</p></p>
